Liv Boeree covers some biases that show up in our day-to-day lives that we often can see in poker.  
  
## Confirmation Bias  
>   
  the most damaging bias that can come upin poker and I also think very often inlife is what's known as confirmationbias- From [[Readwise/Articles/4 Professional Poker Lessons to Help You Think Clearly and Live Wisely Liv Boeree Big Think.md|4 Professional Poker Lessons to Help You Think Clearly and Live Wisely Liv Boeree Big Think]]  
  
The desire to want something to be true and to overvalue evidence that would confirm that, and undervalue evidence that disproves it.  
  
## Status Quo Bias  
>   
the past and this is my way of doingthings- From [[Readwise/Articles/4 Professional Poker Lessons to Help You Think Clearly and Live Wisely Liv Boeree Big Think.md|4 Professional Poker Lessons to Help You Think Clearly and Live Wisely Liv Boeree Big Think]]  
  
In poker this could mean playing with a certain style that doesn't account for the other players.  
  
## Sunk Cost Fallacy  
>   
often go to ourselves oh well I put I'vegone this far I've put this much in Imight as well see it through to the end- From [[Readwise/Articles/4 Professional Poker Lessons to Help You Think Clearly and Live Wisely Liv Boeree Big Think.md|4 Professional Poker Lessons to Help You Think Clearly and Live Wisely Liv Boeree Big Think]]  
  
A very common situation where we fail to consider new information.  
  
## Emotional Difference  
>   
  it'sinteresting to observe sort of thedifference in emotion you can have whenyou end up in a very similar situation- From [[Readwise/Articles/4 Professional Poker Lessons to Help You Think Clearly and Live Wisely Liv Boeree Big Think.md|4 Professional Poker Lessons to Help You Think Clearly and Live Wisely Liv Boeree Big Think]]  
  
Example she gives is where she loses a number of chips to get to N vs when she's won a hand to get the same number of chips. This gain or loss could affect you emotionally when in reality you have the same number of chips.
